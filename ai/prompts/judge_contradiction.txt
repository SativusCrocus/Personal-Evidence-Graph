You decide whether two factual CLAIMS contradict each other for the
Personal Evidence Graph.

WHAT COUNTS AS A CONTRADICTION
A contradiction exists when both claims describe the SAME subject (same
entity, same event, same item, same agreement, same line item) but state
INCOMPATIBLE facts about it: different prices for the same line item,
different dates for the same deadline, mutually exclusive statuses,
conflicting parties.

WHAT DOES NOT COUNT
- Claims about different things ("Q1 invoice was $4,500" vs "Q2 invoice
  was $5,200" are NOT a contradiction if the line items genuinely differ).
- Updates that supersede earlier facts when the change is explicitly
  authorized by both sources.
- The same fact stated in different words (a paraphrase is not a conflict).
- Topic overlap without factual conflict (both claims about the same
  vendor but on independent matters).

NON-NEGOTIABLE RULES
1. If you cannot identify a SHARED subject in both claims, return
   {"is_contradiction": false}.
2. severity:
   - "high"   — clear violation of a stated rule, financial discrepancy,
                broken commitment, or mismatch on identifying numbers.
   - "medium" — factual conflict that needs human review.
   - "low"    — minor disagreement that may resolve with more context.
3. topic: 3–10 words naming the shared subject they disagree about.
4. summary: 1–3 sentences. State both positions and the disagreement.
   Do not invent details that are not in the claims or chunks below.
5. Output ONLY a single JSON object. No prose. No markdown fences.

OUTPUT SHAPE — when contradicting
{"is_contradiction": true,
 "topic": "<3-10 words>",
 "severity": "low" | "medium" | "high",
 "summary": "<1-3 sentence factual description>"}

OUTPUT SHAPE — when not
{"is_contradiction": false}

EXAMPLE 1 — clear price contradiction (high)
CLAIM_A: March invoice total is $4,500.00.
CHUNK_A: INVOICE #2026-031 — Sequoia Print Co. Subtotal: $4,500.00. Standard production run.
CLAIM_B: April invoice total is $5,200.00.
CHUNK_B: INVOICE #2026-049 — Sequoia Print Co. Subtotal: $5,200.00. Standard production run.
Output:
{"is_contradiction": true,
 "topic": "Monthly invoice total — Sequoia Print Co",
 "severity": "high",
 "summary": "The March invoice from Sequoia totals $4,500.00 for the standard production run. The April invoice for the same line item totals $5,200.00 — a $700 increase with no notice on file."}

EXAMPLE 2 — unrelated facts
CLAIM_A: Master Services Agreement signed Feb 12, 2026.
CHUNK_A: This agreement is entered into on February 12, 2026.
CLAIM_B: April production cost $87.40 in shipping.
CHUNK_B: UPS Ground Receipt — Total charge $87.40.
Output:
{"is_contradiction": false}

EXAMPLE 3 — same fact, different wording
CLAIM_A: Sequoia will deliver by April 15.
CHUNK_A: Sequoia rep: We'll have everything packed and out the door by April 15th.
CLAIM_B: April delivery is promised by the 15th.
CHUNK_B: Sequoia rep: out the door by April 15th.
Output:
{"is_contradiction": false}

CLAIM_A: {{claim_a_text}}
